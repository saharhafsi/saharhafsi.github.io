Profile

About

I am a PhD candidate specializing in multimodal artificial intelligence for computer-aided detection of neurodegenerative diseases. My research focuses on combining complementary digital biomarkers extracted from speech and eye-movement signals to improve the detection of Parkinson's disease.

I develop complete machine learning pipelines covering multimodal data acquisition, signal preprocessing, biomarker extraction, feature selection, age-related adjustment, classification, and multimodal fusion. My work investigates feature-level and decision-level fusion strategies, including weighted late fusion, to determine how heterogeneous clinical signals can be integrated effectively.

Particular attention is given to model interpretability, robust evaluation on limited clinical datasets, and the translation of multimodal AI methods into practical clinical decision-support tools. This research is conducted at the Signals & Smart Systems Laboratory at ENIT, in collaboration with Université Paris Cité and clinical partners at Razi Hospital.

Research

Multimodal research focus

My work studies each biomedical modality independently and then examines how their complementary information can be integrated to improve clinical prediction.

Speech Signal Analysis

Extraction and analysis of acoustic biomarkers from sustained vowels and speech, including MFCC, PLP, jitter, shimmer, HNR, and voice regularity measures.

Oculomotor Signal Analysis

Characterization of saccades, antisaccades, smooth pursuit, spontaneous blinks, fixations, and Square-Wave Jerks from eye-tracking and videonystagmography data.

Core research area

Multimodal Data Fusion

Development and evaluation of feature-level and decision-level fusion strategies for integrating speech and oculomotor biomarkers. This includes Early Fusion, conventional Late Fusion, and optimized Weighted Late Fusion for robust and interpretable clinical classification.

Multimodal framework

From heterogeneous signals to clinical prediction

The proposed workflow integrates complementary speech and oculomotor information through a structured, reproducible, and interpretable machine learning pipeline.
